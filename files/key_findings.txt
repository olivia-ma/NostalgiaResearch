
============================================================
KEY FINDINGS: NOSTALGIA VS COLLECTIBLE BUYERS
============================================================

SAMPLE SIZE:
  Total reviews analyzed: 44,469
  - Nostalgic:   6,622 (14.9%)
  - Collectible: 34,130 (76.8%)
  - Both:        3,717 (8.4%)

============================================================
1. PLAYTIME PATTERNS
============================================================

Median hours played:
  - Nostalgic:   5.00 hours
  - Collectible: 4.47 hours
  - Both:        6.55 hours

Mean hours played:
  - Nostalgic:   27.57 hours
  - Collectible: 24.12 hours
  - Both:        25.83 hours

INTERPRETATION:
  → Nostalgic buyers show HIGHER engagement with games
  → Collectible buyers may purchase with LOW intent to play

============================================================
2. GAME AGE AT REVIEW
============================================================

Average years since release:
  - Nostalgic:   6.83 years
  - Collectible: 4.73 years
  - Both:        6.09 years

INTERPRETATION:
  → Nostalgic buyers review OLDER games on average
  → Time since release IS a strong differentiator

============================================================
3. SENTIMENT (RECOMMENDATION RATE)
============================================================

% of reviews that recommend the game:
  - Nostalgic:   86.0%
  - Collectible: 70.0%
  - Both:        77.0%

INTERPRETATION:
  → Nostalgic buyers are MORE likely to recommend
  → Sentiment difference: 16.0 percentage points

============================================================
4. REVIEW BEHAVIOR
============================================================

Average review length (words):
  - Nostalgic:   91.9 words
  - Collectible: 170.6 words
  - Both:        363.6 words

INTERPRETATION:
  → Collectible buyers write longer reviews
  → Review length difference: 78.8 words

============================================================
BUSINESS IMPLICATIONS
============================================================

For game publishers and marketers:

1. NOSTALGIC BUYERS:
   - High engagement, emotional connection to games
   - Positive sentiment overall
   - Marketing angle: emotional storytelling, "relive your childhood"
   - Product strategy: remasters, sequels to classic franchises

2. COLLECTIBLE BUYERS:
   - Lower playtime vs nostalgic buyers
   - May purchase for investment/preservation
   - Marketing angle: limited editions, special packaging
   - Product strategy: physical media, collector's editions

3. OVERLAP ("BOTH"):
   - Represents 3,717 reviews (8.4%)
   - Combines emotional + collecting motivations
   - Highest value segment for premium offerings
